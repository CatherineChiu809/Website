9/21/23 - The week of September 11th, we started to create our cad design out of cardboard and tape. On Monday the 11th, we started by measuring and drawing out the heights for the columns, pulleys, levers, and tracks. Then on Wednesday, we started to cut out and attach the parts to the cardboard base. We ran into our first problem with the pulley. The single marble that we had planned to use did not weigh enough for the basket on the pulley to be pulled down. Also, the pulley kept getting caught on itself and would not move. We decided to add more marbles to increase the weight and made a pulley with the rope on an incline so it would move easier. This solved the pulley problem!! Sort of at least... The plan was to have the pulley lift a platform so that another marble would roll down the rest of the track and eventually cause the balloon to pop. The pulley still did not have enough force to hit the track hard enough to make the marble move. Instead, the marble hugged the wall that we made and got stuck each time. On Friday, we came up with a solution. We added a wedge to space the marble from the wall so it would be bumped off more easily. This helped the marble move and the problem was solved. Next on Monday the 18th, we needed to create the lever system that would pop the balloon, but the marble did not hit the lever with enough force to pop it. Therefore, we changed marble sizes and ramp inclines and then the marble hit hard enough. On Wednesday, we did our final tests and completed the project; finally!! 10/6/23 - Last week was the first week of bioengineering. On Monday we went over the slides for the unit and learned that there are many subcategories of bioengineering. For example there is biomechanics which is about structure of the mechanical aspects of biological systems, genetic and tissue engineering which is about making Artificial Organs, DNA technology, and medical devices engineering, which makes medical devices for hospitals. For our lab this week we made a prosthetic hand out of cardboard, tape, and string. Our design had slightly cut creases for easily bending joints, string for ligaments and tendons, and tape to hold it all together. In the second design that is not pictured here, we threaded the strings through the back of the hand so they could be accessed more easily.





9/20/23 - This week is the areospace engineering unit. We learned that there are two different branches of areospace engineering areonautics and astronautics. Areonautics is for things in flight inside of Earth's atmosphere and astronautics is for things in flight outside of Earth's atmosphere. For this unit we made bottle rockets made out of two liter bottles, cardboard, and paper. We made our water bottle rocket out of two bottles for an elongated shape, a nose cone for areodinamics, and fins for stablity. Unfortunately, when we tested, the rocket was too top heavy so it did not go very far and it flipped many times and ended up going backward. I learned that while having a rocket that is long and areodinamic is important, it is important to balance the weight as well.





9/26/23 - We started the envernmental engineering unit this week. We learned about many of the things envernmental engineers can study like water filtration, global warming, water pollution, and light pollution. For the lab this week we made water filtration devices out of bottles, cotton balls, sand, and rocks. I learned that you should wash the rocks and sand because they already have a lot of dirt on them. Then you should have a filter that works slower and more effiently so you don't have to send it back through the filter many times. Our filter was made of three parts as to best clear the water. The top part had large rocks to stop the large chunks of dirt from going through, then some sand, medium rocks, more sand and cotton balls. We used the cotton balls to stop small holes we made in the cup to keep large particals out of the water. We then had two more half bottle with holes poked in the caps with sand, large rocks, medium rocks, and cotton balls which let out into a cup. The filtered water was light yellow, so a definite improvment. Below are the pictures of the filter and the difference between the dirty water and the clean water.

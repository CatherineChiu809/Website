9/21/23 - The week of September 11th, we started to create our cad design out of cardboard and tape. On Monday the 11th, we started by measuring and drawing out the hights for the columns, pully, levers, and tracks. Then on Wendesday, we started to cut out and attach the parts to the cardboard base. We ran into our first problem with the pully. The one marble that we had planned to use did not weigh enough for basket on the pully to be pulled down also the pully kept getting caught on itself and would not move. We decided to add more marbles to increase the weight and made a pully with the rope on an incline so it would move easier. This solved the pully problem!! Sort of at least... The plan was to have the pully lift a platform so that another marble would roll down the rest of the track and eventually cause the balloon to pop. The pully still did not have enough force to hit the track hard enough to make the marble move. Instead, the marble hugged the wall that we made and got stuck each time. On Friday, we came up with a sollution. We addded a wedge to space the marble from the wall so it would be bumped off easier. This helped the marble move and the problem was solved. Next on Monday the 18th, we needed to create the lever system that would pop the balloon, but the marble did not hit the leaver with enough force to pop it. Therefore, we changed marble sizes and ramp inclines and then the marble hit hard enough. On Wenesday, we did our final tests and completed the project; finally!! 10/6/23 - Last week was the first week of bioengeering. On Monday we went over the slides for the unit and learned that they are many subcatogories of bioengeering. For example there is biomechanics which is about structure of the mechanical aspects of biological systems, genetic and tissue engineering which is about making Artificial Organs, DNA technology, and medical devices engieering, which makes medical devices for hospitals. For our lab this week we made a prosthetic hand out of cardboard, tape, and string. Our design had slightly cut creases for easily bending joints, string for ligaments and tendons, and tape to hold it all together. In the second design that is not pictured here, we threaded the strings through the back of the hand so they could be accessed more easily.

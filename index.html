1/10/24 - Our final this semester was based on civil engineering. The challenge was to make a 2 foot long bridge out of 40 pieces of spaghetti. We had to make two iterations and improve our design over the course of the process. Below is an analysis of our final.

Civil Engineering - Spaghetti Bridge Analysis
  1. Our first prototype bridge weighed 30.9 g. Our final bridge, however, weighed 29.6 g.
  2. Our prototype bridge held about 543.84 g. Our final bridge held 1263 g.
  3. The final score of the first bridge was 17.6 and the final score of the second bridge was 42.6.
  4. Most of the weight from both of our bridges came from the glue.
  5. The inspiration for our bridge came from researching types of bridges and what make them able to support a heavy load. We learned that crisscrossing triangles, thick structures, and tight features would hold our bridge up.
  6. Our initial bridge did not work as well as we intended but, in retrospect, it makes sense because we made the bridge very thin which did not give it much support. Our second bridge preformed much better than we expected because after the first test we were not very confident. As it turns out, because we made the layered the spaghetti to make it taller and thicker and used less glue to make it more flexable, it was able to hold a lot more weight.
  7. The points of failure were in the same on both bridges. While we were expecting them at the middle, but the points of failure were actually directly next to where we were holding the bridge.
  8. To improve our bridge and prolong these point of failure, we could add extra flexablity and support to the weak areas of the bridge.
